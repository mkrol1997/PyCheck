What are checkers?

Checkers, also called Draughts, is a group of strategy board games for two players. American Checkers are among the two most popular variants of Checkers.

How to play Checkers?

In American variant of Checkers ordinary pieces move diagonally forward. When it comes to capturing opponents' pieces, there are some differences...

Which color starts the game?

One player has the white pieces and the other plays with the black pieces. In American Checkers the first move belongs to the player with the light ones. The game starts automatically when both users are connected.

Board size!

In American Checkers, known also as English Draughts the board consists of 64 squares (8x8 checkerboard).

In American Checkers capturing is obligatory, but you can decide which of the available capturing possibilities to choose from. In International Draughts you always have to jump over the highest number of pieces possible. It is the so-called majority rule. In American Checkers pieces cannot take backwards. The only exception is a king.

King Pawn?

When an uncrowned piece reaches the king row it turns into a king. In American Checkers a king moves only one square, but unlike regular pieces, a king can move and capture backwards, too.
